# Harbor Vault Business Strategy Index

This folder contains business and go-to-market strategy documents for Harbor Vault.

RFCs define what Codex should build.

Business strategy documents define why the product exists, who it serves, how it is positioned, and how Cottonwood Harbor will bring it to market.

## Strategy Tracks

Harbor Vault should evolve across three connected tracks:

- The Product: RFCs, architecture, specifications, implementation.
- The Business: pricing, positioning, target market, launch plan, sales assets.
- The Company: Cottonwood Harbor brand, mission, values, standards, long-term vision.

These should evolve together.

## Guiding Narrative

Harbor Vault is the operating system for small Organizations.

Its product commandment:

Power without complexity.

The simplest solution that solves the problem wins.

Its core value proposition:

One Organization.

One Platform.

One Source of Truth.

One Monthly Price.

Not AI project management.

Not a better Notion.

Not another task manager.

An operating system connects everything:

- Organizations
- Work
- Documents
- Harbor AI
- Calendar
- Reports
- Search

Every business strategy document should reinforce that narrative unless customer validation proves a better one.
